REAL MADRID

It was officially opened on September 30, 2005. Many call it Valdebebas due to its location in the Valdebebas district of Madrid. The project was designed by architect Carlos Lamela, and the inauguration was attended by Real Madrid's Honorary President, Alfredo Di Stéfano, and Emilio Butragueño, Head of Protocol and Public Relations. The sports city covers approximately 1,067 hectares of land, and part of Madrid-Barajas Airport can be seen from its facilities. The sports city covers an area of ​​approximately 1,200,000 square meters, which is ten times the size of the previous sports city and 40 times the size of the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium. The massive T-shaped building in the sports city covers an area of ​​9,000 square meters and includes changing rooms, gymnasiums, classrooms, conference rooms, offices, hydrotherapy and medical treatment centers, press rooms, and more. On either side of the building are 10 football fields covered with natural and artificial grass, surrounded by stands that can accommodate more than 11,000 fans. see more ...
